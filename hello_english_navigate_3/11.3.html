Reading and Listening Text

In 2014, a young journalist called Nina started working for an online news website that published short articles, quizzes, and listicles. At first, she thought listicles were too simple to be real journalism.

En 2014, una joven periodista llamada Nina empezó a trabajar para un website de noticias en línea que publicaba artículos cortos, cuestionarios y listicles. Al principio, pensó que los listicles eran demasiado simples para ser verdadero journalism.

She preferred long political articles, detailed interviews, and serious reports about international events. However, after a few months, she noticed something important: people were not only reading listicles because they were fun; they were reading them because they were manageable.

Ella prefería artículos políticos largos, entrevistas detalladas e informes serios sobre eventos internacionales. Sin embargo, después de unos meses, notó algo importante: las personas no solo leían listicles porque eran divertidos; los leían porque eran manejables.

Every morning, Nina watched people on the train looking at their phones. Some were tired, some were busy, and some had only five minutes before work.

Cada mañana, Nina veía a personas en el tren mirando sus teléfonos. Algunas estaban cansadas, algunas estaban ocupadas y algunas solo tenían cinco minutos antes del trabajo.

A long article, which required quiet time and deep attention, was not always possible. A listicle, which gave clear signposts and short sections, helped readers understand information quickly. Nina realized that this format could be useful, especially when the topic was difficult.

Un artículo largo, que requería tiempo tranquilo y atención profunda, no siempre era posible. Un listicle, que daba señales claras y secciones cortas, ayudaba a los lectores a entender la información rápidamente. Nina se dio cuenta de que este formato podía ser útil, especialmente cuando el tema era difícil.

One day, she wrote an article called “Six important facts about plastic in the ocean.” It had a simple structure, but the information was reliable and relevant. Each point explained one problem and one possible solution.

Un día, escribió un artículo llamado “Seis datos importantes sobre el plástico en el océano.” Tenía una estructura simple, pero la información era reliable y relevant. Cada punto explicaba un problema y una posible solución.

The article became popular because it was informative without being overwhelming. Teachers shared it with students, environmental groups posted it online, and readers commented that it had helped them understand the issue.

El artículo se volvió popular porque era informative sin ser abrumador. Los profesores lo compartieron con estudiantes, grupos environmental lo publicaron en línea y los lectores comentaron que les había ayudado a entender el problema.

Nina still believed that traditional journalism was valuable, but she no longer thought listicles were pointless. She learned that good writing depends on purpose.

Nina todavía creía que el journalism tradicional era valioso, pero ya no pensaba que los listicles no tenían sentido. Aprendió que la buena escritura depende del propósito.

If the purpose is to explain complex information clearly, a list can be an effective tool. If the purpose is to investigate a serious problem in depth, a longer article may be better. In the end, Nina concluded that the format was less important than the quality, accuracy, and honesty of the content.

Si el propósito es explicar información compleja claramente, una lista puede ser una herramienta effective. Si el propósito es investigar un problema serio en profundidad, un artículo más largo puede ser mejor. Al final, Nina concluyó que el formato era menos importante que la calidad, precisión y honestidad del contenido.

Grammar: Understanding Complex Sentences

Now let’s move to grammar: understanding complex sentences. A complex sentence has more than one idea. The key is to quickly identify the subject, the verb, and the object or complement.

Ahora pasemos a la gramática: entender complex sentences. Una oración compleja tiene más de una idea. La clave es identificar rápidamente el sujeto, el verbo y el objeto o complemento.
  • For example: “Nina wrote an article.” Nina is the subject, wrote is the verb, and an article is the object.Por ejemplo: “Nina wrote an article.” Nina es el sujeto, wrote es el verbo y an article es el objeto.

Now add extra information: “Nina, who worked for an online news website, wrote an article that became popular.” The main idea is still simple: Nina wrote an article. “Who worked for an online news website” gives extra information about Nina. “That became popular” gives extra information about the article.

Ahora añade información extra: “Nina, who worked for an online news website, wrote an article that became popular.” La idea principal sigue siendo simple: Nina escribió un artículo. “Who worked for an online news website” da información extra sobre Nina. “That became popular” da información extra sobre el artículo.

Exactly. Complex sentences often include relative clauses. Listen: “A listicle, which gives information in short sections, can help busy readers.” The main idea is: A listicle can help busy readers. The clause “which gives information in short sections” adds detail.

Exactamente. Las complex sentences a menudo incluyen relative clauses. Escucha: “A listicle, which gives information in short sections, can help busy readers.” La idea principal es: un listicle puede ayudar a lectores ocupados. La cláusula “which gives information in short sections” añade detalle.

They can also include participle clauses. For example: “Founded in 2006, the website became famous quickly.” This means: The website was founded in 2006, and it became famous quickly. Another example: “Designed for quick reading, the article was easy to follow.”

También pueden incluir participle clauses. Por ejemplo: “Founded in 2006, the website became famous quickly.” Esto significa: El website fue fundado en 2006 y se volvió famoso rápidamente. Otro ejemplo: “Designed for quick reading, the article was easy to follow.”

Let’s practise with examples.

Practiquemos con ejemplos.
  • “Readers using their phones often prefer short articles.” The main idea is: Readers prefer short articles. “Using their phones” adds information about the readers.“Readers using their phones often prefer short articles.” La idea principal es: Los lectores prefieren artículos cortos. “Using their phones” añade información sobre los lectores.
  • “Stories shared on social media can become viral.” The main idea is: Stories can become viral. “Shared on social media” adds detail.“Stories shared on social media can become viral.” La idea principal es: Las historias pueden volverse viral. “Shared on social media” añade detalle.

Vocabulary: Adjective Suffixes

Now let’s move to vocabulary: adjective suffixes. A suffix is added to the end of a word. The suffix -al often forms adjectives from nouns: accident becomes accidental, environment becomes environmental, tradition becomes traditional, politics becomes political.

Ahora pasemos al vocabulario: adjective suffixes. Un sufijo se añade al final de una palabra. El sufijo -al a menudo forma adjetivos a partir de sustantivos: accident se convierte en accidental, environment se convierte en environmental, tradition se convierte en traditional, politics se convierte en political.

The suffix -able often means “can be done” or “having a quality.” Manage becomes manageable. Rely becomes reliable. Share becomes shareable. Believe becomes believable.

El sufijo -able a menudo significa “que puede hacerse” o “que tiene una cualidad.” Manage se convierte en manageable. Rely se convierte en reliable. Share se convierte en shareable. Believe se convierte en believable.
  • For example: This article is manageable.Por ejemplo: Este artículo es manageable.
  • That source is reliable.Esa fuente es reliable.
  • The video is shareable.El video es shareable.

The suffix -ive often forms adjectives from verbs: inform becomes informative, create becomes creative, attract becomes attractive, effect becomes effective.

El sufijo -ive a menudo forma adjetivos a partir de verbos: inform se convierte en informative, create se convierte en creative, attract se convierte en attractive, effect se convierte en effective.
  • Example: The report was informative.Ejemplo: El informe fue informative.
  • Her headline was creative.Su headline fue creative.
  • The structure was effective.La estructura fue effective.

The suffix -ant can also form adjectives: importance gives important, relevance gives relevant, dominance gives dominant.

El sufijo -ant también puede formar adjetivos: importance da important, relevance da relevant, dominance da dominant.
  • Example: The topic is relevant to students.Ejemplo: El tema es relevant para los estudiantes.
  • The main idea is important.La idea principal es important.
  • That website is dominant in online entertainment.Ese website es dominant en online entertainment.

Ahora pasemos a la pronunciación. Escucha el word stress. “Informative” es /ɪnˈfɔːmətɪv/: in-FOR-ma-tive. “Reliable” es /rɪˈlaɪəbl/: re-LI-a-ble. “Manageable” es /ˈmænɪdʒəbl/: MAN-a-gea-ble. “Environmental” es /ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmentl/: en-vi-ron-MEN-tal. “Relevant” es /ˈreləvənt/: REL-e-vant.
