Useful Vocabulary

Exactly! Let’s begin with useful vocabulary. A “drama series” tells an emotional story over many episodes. A “sitcom” is a funny programme with the same characters in different situations. A “current affairs programme” discusses important news and social topics.

¡Exactamente! Empecemos con vocabulario útil. Una “drama series” cuenta una historia emocional a lo largo de muchos episodios. Una “sitcom” es un programa divertido con los mismos personajes en diferentes situaciones. Un “current affairs programme” trata noticias importantes y temas sociales.

A “cookery programme” teaches people how to cook. A “home improvement programme” shows people repairing or changing houses. A “sports programme” shows games, competitions, or match highlights. A “gripping plot” is a story that keeps your attention. “Commercial breaks” are short adverts during a programme.

Un “cookery programme” enseña a las personas cómo cocinar. Un “home improvement programme” muestra a personas reparando o cambiando casas. Un “sports programme” muestra juegos, competiciones o resúmenes de partidos. Un “gripping plot” es una historia que mantiene tu atención. Los “Commercial breaks” son anuncios cortos durante un programa.

¡Muy bien! Ahora pasemos a nuestra historia para leer y escuchar. Escucha el vocabulario de televisión y ejemplos de reported speech.

Reading and Listening Story

On Friday evening, Lucas planned to watch one episode of a new drama series called Dark Harbour. His sister Elena looked at him and said, “Don’t start it too late.” Lucas laughed and said that he was only going to watch one episode.

El viernes por la noche, Lucas planeó ver un episodio de una nueva drama series llamada Dark Harbour. Su hermana Elena lo miró y dijo: “No la empieces demasiado tarde.” Lucas se rió y dijo que solo iba a ver un episodio.

At nine o’clock, the first episode began with a mysterious phone call, a missing boat, and a detective with a complicated past. The plot was gripping, and Lucas wanted to know what happened next.

A las nueve en punto, el primer episodio comenzó con una llamada telefónica misteriosa, un bote desaparecido y un detective con un pasado complicado. La trama era gripping, y Lucas quería saber qué pasaba después.

When the episode finished, the platform automatically showed the next one. Lucas said, “Fine, just one more.”

Cuando el episodio terminó, la plataforma mostró automáticamente el siguiente. Lucas dijo: “Está bien, solo uno más.”

At midnight, Elena came into the living room and asked him if he was still awake. Lucas said that he was nearly finished, but he had already watched four episodes. Elena told him that he looked exhausted.

A medianoche, Elena entró en la sala y le preguntó si todavía estaba despierto. Lucas dijo que casi había terminado, pero ya había visto cuatro episodios. Elena le dijo que se veía agotado.

Lucas admitted that the series was more addictive than he had expected. He said the actors were excellent and the story was full of surprising twists.

Lucas admitió que la serie era más adictiva de lo que había esperado. Dijo que los actores eran excelentes y que la historia estaba llena de giros sorprendentes.

Elena asked whether it was really worth losing sleep. Lucas said he didn’t know, but he wanted to discover who had taken the boat.

Elena preguntó si realmente valía la pena perder sueño. Lucas dijo que no lo sabía, pero quería descubrir quién se había llevado el bote.

The next morning, Lucas felt like a couch potato. He had breakfast slowly and missed his football practice. Elena asked him what he had learned.

A la mañana siguiente, Lucas se sintió como un couch potato. Desayunó lentamente y se perdió su práctica de fútbol. Elena le preguntó qué había aprendido.

Lucas said that streaming was convenient, but it could easily transform a relaxed evening into a long night. Later, he made a simple rule: if he watched a series during the week, he would stop after one episode.

Lucas dijo que el streaming era conveniente, pero que podía transformar fácilmente una noche relajada en una noche larga. Más tarde, hizo una regla simple: si veía una serie durante la semana, se detendría después de un episodio.

Elena smiled and said that this sounded sensible. Lucas replied that he would try, unless the next episode ended with a huge mystery.

Elena sonrió y dijo que eso sonaba sensato. Lucas respondió que lo intentaría, a menos que el siguiente episodio terminara con un gran misterio.

Grammar: Reported Speech

Now let’s move to grammar: reported speech. Reported speech tells another person what someone said.

Ahora pasemos a la gramática: reported speech. El reported speech le dice a otra persona lo que alguien dijo.
  • Direct speech: Lucas said, “I am only going to watch one episode.”Direct speech: Lucas dijo: “Solo voy a ver un episodio.”
  • Reported speech: Lucas said that he was only going to watch one episode.Reported speech: Lucas dijo que solo iba a ver un episodio.

When we report something said in the past, the verb often moves one step back.

Cuando reportamos algo dicho en el pasado, el verbo a menudo retrocede un paso.
  • “I am tired” becomes “He said that he was tired.”“I am tired” se convierte en “He said that he was tired.”
  • “I watch dramas” becomes “She said that she watched dramas.”“I watch dramas” se convierte en “She said that she watched dramas.”
  • “I have finished the episode” becomes “He said that he had finished the episode.”“I have finished the episode” se convierte en “He said that he had finished the episode.”

More examples.

Más ejemplos.
  • Direct: “The programme is amazing.” Reported: She said that the programme was amazing.Direct: “The programme is amazing.” Reported: Ella dijo que el programme era increíble.
  • Direct: “I watched a sitcom yesterday.” Reported: He said that he had watched a sitcom the previous day.Direct: “I watched a sitcom yesterday.” Reported: Él dijo que había visto una sitcom el día anterior.
  • Direct: “I will bring the DVD tomorrow.” Reported: She said that she would bring the DVD the next day.Direct: “I will bring the DVD tomorrow.” Reported: Ella dijo que traería el DVD al día siguiente.

Excellent. Time words often change too.

Excelente. Las palabras de tiempo a menudo también cambian.
  • “Today” can become “that day.”“Today” puede convertirse en “that day.”
  • “Yesterday” becomes “the previous day.”“Yesterday” se convierte en “the previous day.”
  • “Tomorrow” becomes “the next day.”“Tomorrow” se convierte en “the next day.”
  • “Now” becomes “then.”“Now” se convierte en “then.”
  • “Last night” becomes “the night before.”“Last night” se convierte en “the night before.”

For example: Elena said, “You looked tired last night.” Elena said that Lucas had looked tired the night before.

Por ejemplo: Elena dijo: “Te veías cansado anoche.” Elena dijo que Lucas se había visto cansado la noche anterior.

Reported Questions

What about questions? In reported questions, the word order changes.

¿Qué pasa con las preguntas? En reported questions, el orden de las palabras cambia.
  • Direct question: “Are you OK?” Reported question: She asked if he was OK.Direct question: “Are you OK?” Reported question: Ella preguntó si él estaba bien.
  • Not “She asked was he OK.”No “She asked was he OK.”
  • Direct: “Did you watch the match?” Reported: He asked whether I had watched the match.Direct: “Did you watch the match?” Reported: Él preguntó si yo había visto el partido.

For question words, use the question word, but keep normal sentence order.

Para question words, usa la question word, pero mantén el orden normal de la oración.
  • Direct: “Where do you watch TV?” Reported: She asked where I watched TV.Direct: “Where do you watch TV?” Reported: Ella preguntó dónde veía TV.
  • Direct: “Why did you binge-watch?” Reported: He asked why I had binge-watched.Direct: “Why did you binge-watch?” Reported: Él preguntó por qué yo había hecho binge-watch.

Speaking

Now let’s move to speaking. Ask someone about their viewing habits. You can say:

Ahora pasemos al speaking. Pregúntale a alguien sobre sus viewing habits. Puedes decir:
  • How much TV do you watch?¿Cuánta TV ves?
  • Do you ever binge-watch?¿Alguna vez haces binge-watch?
  • What programmes do you prefer?¿Qué programmes prefieres?
  • Do you watch alone or with others?¿Ves solo o con otras personas?
  • What is the best series you have seen recently?¿Cuál es la mejor series que has visto recientemente?

Then report the answers. Example: I asked Mia how much TV she watched. She said that she watched about five hours a week. She told me that she preferred sitcoms because they helped her relax. She said she had never binge-watched a whole season.

Luego reporta las respuestas. Example: Le pregunté a Mia cuánta TV veía. Ella dijo que veía unas cinco horas a la semana. Me dijo que prefería sitcoms porque la ayudaban a relajarse. Dijo que nunca había hecho binge-watched una temporada completa.

Writing

Now let’s move to writing. Write a short paragraph reporting a friend’s TV habits. Include three reported statements and one reported question.

Ahora pasemos al writing. Escribe un párrafo corto reportando los TV habits de un amigo. Incluye tres reported statements y una reported question.

Example: I asked Leo what he usually watched. He said that he enjoyed sports programmes and documentaries. He told me that he had watched match highlights the previous night. He said that streaming was useful because he could watch without commercial breaks.

Example: Le pregunté a Leo qué veía normalmente. Dijo que disfrutaba sports programmes y documentaries. Me dijo que había visto match highlights la noche anterior. Dijo que el streaming era útil porque podía ver sin commercial breaks.

Review and Closing

Let’s review. Binge-watching means watching many episodes one after another. A couch potato is someone who spends too much time sitting and watching TV. Reported speech changes verbs, pronouns, and time words when we report past conversations.

Repasemos. Binge-watching significa ver muchos episodios uno tras otro. Un couch potato es alguien que pasa demasiado tiempo sentado y viendo TV. Reported speech cambia verbos, pronombres y palabras de tiempo cuando reportamos conversaciones pasadas.
