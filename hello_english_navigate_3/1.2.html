Grammar: Present Perfect Simple & Continuous

Gramática: Presente Perfecto Simple y Continuo

Now let’s pass to grammar, because this topic is perfect for the present perfect simple and the present perfect continuous.

Ahora pasemos a la gramática, porque este tema es perfecto para el presente perfecto simple y el presente perfecto continuo.

First, the present perfect simple. We form it with have or has plus the past participle. For example: I have written three emails today. She has kept all her old letters. We use it to talk about experience, completed actions with a result now, and actions at an unspecified time connected to the present.

Primero, el presente perfecto simple. Lo formamos con have o has más el participio pasado. Por ejemplo: He escrito tres correos electrónicos hoy. Ella ha guardado todas sus cartas viejas. Lo usamos para hablar de experiencias, acciones completadas con un resultado ahora y acciones en un momento no especificado conectadas con el presente.

Listen carefully. I have received many emails this week. He has lost the address. We have never written to that company before. In these examples, the result or experience is important now.

Escucha con atención. He recibido muchos correos electrónicos esta semana. Él ha perdido la dirección. Nunca antes habíamos escrito a esa empresa. En estos ejemplos, el resultado o la experiencia es importante ahora.

Now the present perfect continuous. We form it with have or has been plus the verb in ing. For example: I have been writing thank-you notes all afternoon. She has been waiting for a reply since Monday. We use it for actions that started in the past and continue now, or repeated actions over a period of time.

Ahora el presente perfecto continuo. Lo formamos con have o has been más el verbo en ing. Por ejemplo: He estado escribiendo notas de agradecimiento toda la tarde. Ella ha estado esperando una respuesta desde el lunes. Lo usamos para acciones que comenzaron en el pasado y continúan ahora, o acciones repetidas durante un período de tiempo.

Here is the important difference. I have written five letters focuses on the result, the number. I have been writing letters for two hours focuses on the activity and duration. One asks, what has happened? The other asks, how long has the activity been happening?

Aquí está la diferencia importante. "I have written five letters" se centra en el resultado, el número. "I have been writing letters for two hours" se centra en la actividad y la duración. Uno pregunta, ¿qué ha pasado? El otro pregunta, ¿cuánto tiempo ha estado ocurriendo la actividad?

We also use since and for. Since gives a starting point: since yesterday, since January, since breakfast. For gives a period of time: for two days, for a month, for years. Example: I have lived here for ten years. I have been studying English since 2024.

También usamos since y for. Since da un punto de partida: desde ayer, desde enero, desde el desayuno. For da un período de tiempo: por dos días, por un mes, por años. Ejemplo: He vivido aquí durante diez años. He estado estudiando inglés desde 2024.

And remember this: some verbs often sound more natural in the simple form when they describe states, like know, have, and be. For example: I have known her for years. Not usually, I have been knowing her.

Y recuerda esto: algunos verbos suelen sonar más naturales en la forma simple cuando describen estados, como know, have y be. Por ejemplo: La conozco desde hace años. No usualmente, "I have been knowing her".

Pronunciation

Pronunciación

Now let’s move into pronunciation. Today we focus on the auxiliary verbs have and been. In connected speech, have is often weak: /həv/ or /əv/. So I have written can sound like /aɪ əv ˈrɪtn/.

Ahora pasemos a la pronunciación. Hoy nos centramos en los verbos auxiliares have y been. En el habla conectada, have suele ser débil: /həv/ o /əv/. Así que "I have written" puede sonar como /aɪ əv ˈrɪtn/.

And been may sound like /biːn/ or /bɪn/, depending on the accent. Listen: I have been waiting, /aɪ əv biːn ˈweɪtɪŋ/. Repeat: have been, have been, have been writing.

Y been puede sonar como /biːn/ o /bɪn/, según el acento. Escucha: I have been waiting, /aɪ əv biːn ˈweɪtɪŋ/. Repite: have been, have been, have been writing.
